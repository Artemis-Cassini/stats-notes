1. A **median** of a triangle is a **segment** connecting a **vertex** of a triangle to the **midpoint** of the opposite side. 
2. Sketch the median connecting vertex A to the midpoint of the opposite side in each triangle below. (see canvas)
3. An **altitude** of a triangle is a segment from a vertex of a triangle perpendicular to the opposite side or the line containing the opposite side. 
4. Draw the altitude from vertex A perpendicular to the opposite side of the line containing the opposite side. (see canvas)
5. A **perpendicular bisector** of a side of a triangle is a line (or segment or ray) perpendicular to the given side at its midpoint. 
6. An **angle bisector** of an angle in a triangle is a ray that divides the angle into two congruent angles. 
7. A point lies of the **perpendicular bisector** of a segment *iff* the point is equidistant to the two endpoints of the segment. 
8. A point likes on an **angle bisector** *iff* the point is equidistant to the sides of the angle. 
9. A **Point of Concurrency** is formed when three (or more) lines, rays, or segments intersect in a single point. 
10. (A) The **3 angle bisectors** in a triangle intersect to form the **incenter**. 
11. (B) The **3 perpendicular bisectors** in a triangle intersect to form the **circumcenter**.
12. (C) The **3 medians** in a triangle intersect to form the **centroid**. 
13. (D) The **3 altitudes** in a triangle intersect to form the **orthocenter**.
