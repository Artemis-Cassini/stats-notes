1. Parallel lines are coplanar lines that do not intersect
2. Skew lines are not coplanar, not parallel, and not intersecting
3. Parallel planes do not intersect
- All horizontal planes are parallel, like the ceiling and floor
- Vertical planes MAY be parallel, like the front and back walls
- DO NOT HAVE TO be parallel, like the front and side wall
4. A line is parallel to a plane if the line and the plane do not intersect
5. Two skew lines are never parallel
6. Two parallel lines are always coplanar
7. A line in the plane of the ceiling and a line in the plane of the floor are sometimes parallel
8. Two lines in the plane of the ceiling are never skew
9. A line in the plane of a wall and a line in the plane of the ceiling are
- sometimes parallel, intersecting, and skew
10. **Parallel Post.** - if there is a line and a point not on the line, then there is exactly 1 line through the point parallel to the given line
11. **Perpendicular Post.** - if there is a line and a point not on the line, then there is exactly 1 line through the point perpendicular to the given line
12. A transversal is a line that intersects 2 or more coplanar lines in different points
