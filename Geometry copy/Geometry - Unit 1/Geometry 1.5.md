1. An angle is a figure formed by two rays that have the same endpoint. The two rays are called the sides of the angle, and their common endpoint is the vertex of the angle. 
- An acute angle has a measure between 0 and 90 degrees
- A right angle has a measure of exactly 90 degrees
- An obtuse angle has a measure between 90 and 180 degrees
- A straight angle has a measure of exactly 180 degrees

2. The rules of algebra are known as "properties"
3. In geometry, statements that are assumed to be true without proof are called "postulates"
