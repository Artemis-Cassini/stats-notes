1. **Inductive Reasoning** is used to make a conjecture based upon many observations
2. A **conjecture** is an unproven statement based upon many observations
3. A **counterexample** is an example that is used to show that an if then statement is false. It only takes **one** counterexample to show that a conjecture is false. 
4. A **proof** is a very logical, step-by-step. convincing argument
5. A **theorem** is a statement that has been proven to be true

6. If two angles are complementary to the same angle, then those two angles are congruent
7. If two angles are supplementary to the same angle, then those two angles are congruent
8. Perpendicular lines are two lines that intersect to form right angles
9. Two lines are parallel *iff* they form a linear pair of congruent angles

10. **Midpoint Theorem** - the distance from an endpoint to the midpoint of a segment is half of the total distance 
11. A **midpoint** is a point that divides a segment into two congruent segments 
12. An **angle bisector** is a ray that divides an angle into two congruent angles
13. **Midpoint Theorem** - If M is the midpoint of segment AB, then AM=1/2AB and MB=1/2AB
14. **Angle Bisector Theorem** - If ray BX is the bisector of angle ABC, then the measure of angle 1=1/2 of angle ABC and the measure of angle 2=1/2 the measure of angle ABC
