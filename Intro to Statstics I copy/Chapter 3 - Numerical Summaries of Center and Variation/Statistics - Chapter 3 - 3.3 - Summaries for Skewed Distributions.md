### The Center as the Middle: The Median
- The **median** of a sample of data is the value that would be right in the middle if you were to sort the data from smallest to largest

### Measuring Variability with the Interquartile Range
- The **interquartile range (IQR)** tells us, roughly, how much space the middle 50% of the data occupy

### Calculating the Interquartile Range
- These points are called the **quartiles**, because they cut the distribution into quarters (fourths)
- The **first quartile (Q1)** has roughly one-fourth, or 25% of the observation at or below it
- The **second quartile (Q2)** has about 50% at or below it; actually, Q2 is just another name for the median
- The **third quartile (Q3)** has about 75% of the observations at or below it
