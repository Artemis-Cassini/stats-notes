### The Center as Balancing Point: The Mean
- The **mean** of a collection of data is the arithmetic average

### The Mean in Context
- The mean tells us one answer to the question of what is a typical value for a variable

### Calculating the Mean for Small Data Sets
- To calculate the mean, find the (arithmetic) **average** of the numbers; that is, simply add up all the numbers and divide that sum by the number of observations

$$x = ∑x/n$$
- The mean shown in that formula is sometimes called the **sample mean** in order to make it clear that it is the mean of a collection (or sample) of data

### Calculating the Mean for Larger Data Sets
- It's better to use a computer or a statistical calculator for larger data sets

- The **standard deviation** is a number that measures how far away the typical observation is from the mean

### The Standard Deviation in Context
- The standard deviation is somewhat more abstract, and harder to understand, than the mean

### Calculating the Standard Deviation
$$s = √∑(x-x)^2/n-1$$
- This distance, including the positive or negative sign, is called a **deviation**

### Variance, a Close Relative of the Standard Deviation
- The **variance** is simply the standard deviations square, and it is represented symbolically by s^2
$$s^2 = ∑(x-x)^2/n-1$$
