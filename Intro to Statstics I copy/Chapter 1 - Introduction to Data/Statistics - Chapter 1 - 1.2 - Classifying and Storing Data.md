- These characteristics are called **variables**. Variables are not "unknowns" like those you studied in algebra
- When we work with data, they are grouped into a collection, which we call either a **data set** or a **sample**. The word *sample* is important, because it implies that the data we see are just one part of a bigger picture
- This "bigger picture" is called a **population**

### Two Types of Variables
- **Numerical variables** describe quantities of the objects of interest. The values will be numbers. The weight of an infant is an example of a numerical variable
- **Categorical variables** describe qualities of the objects of interest. These values will be categories. The gender of an infant is an example of a categorical example
