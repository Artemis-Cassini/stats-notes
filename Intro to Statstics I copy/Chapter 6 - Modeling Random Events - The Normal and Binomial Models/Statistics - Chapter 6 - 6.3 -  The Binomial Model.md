- The **binomial probability model** is useful in many situation with discrete-valued numerical variables (typically counts, whole numbers)

### Finding Binomial Probabilities 
- We use the notation *b(n, p, x)* to represent the **binomial probability** of getting *x* successes in a binomial experiment with *n* trials and probability of success *p*

### Finding (Slightly) More Complex Probabilities 
- In general, probabilities of *x* or *fewer* are called **cumulative probabilities** 

### Interpreting the Mean and the Standard Deviation
- The mean of any probability distribution, including the binomial, is sometimes called the **expected value**
