- A **probability distribution**, sometimes called a **probability distribution function (pdf)**, is a tool that helps us by keeping track of the outcomes of a random experiment and the probabilities associated with those outcomes
- Statisticians sometimes refer to probability distributions as **probability models**
- **Discrete outcomes** (or discrete variables) are numerical values that you can list or count
- **Continuous outcomes** (or continuous variables) cannot be listed or counted because they occur over a range

### Continuous Probabilities Are Represented as Areas under Curves
- The curve is called a **probability density curve**
