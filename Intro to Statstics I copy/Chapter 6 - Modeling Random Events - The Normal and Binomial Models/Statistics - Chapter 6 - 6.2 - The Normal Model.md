- The **Normal model** is the most widely used probability model for continuous numerical variables

### Visualizing the Normal Distribution
- The curve drawn on these histograms is called the **Normal curve**, or the **Normal distribution**

### Center and Spread
- The **mean of a probability distribution** is represented by the Greek character μ (mu, pronounced "mew"), and the **standard deviation of a probability distribution** is represented by the character σ (sigma)

### Without Technology - The Standard Normal
- This model--the Normal model with mean - and standard deviation 1--has a special name: the **standard Normal model**

### Finding Measurements from Percentiles for the Normal Distribution
- Such a number is called a **percentile**
