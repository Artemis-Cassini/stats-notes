- When examining a distribution, pay attention to describing the shape of the distribution, the **typical value (center)** of the distribution, and the **variability (spread)** in the distribution

### Shape
- A **symmetric distribution** has one mound (Statisticians often describe a distribution with this particular shape as a **bell-shaped distribution.** Bell-shaped distributions play a major role in statistics, as you will see throughout this text)
- Because the tail goes to the right, we call it a **right-skewed distribution**
- If the tail goes to the left, it is a **left-skewed distribution**, where most values are relatively large but there are also a few very small values
- The statistical term for a one-mound distribution is **unimodal distribution**, and a two-mound distribution is called a **bimodal distribution**
- A **multimodal distribution** has more than two modes
- Extreme values such as these are called **outliers**
