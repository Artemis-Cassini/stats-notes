**Theoretical, Empirical, and Simulation-Based Probabilities
- **Probability** is used to measure how often random events occur
- The first is called **theoretical probability**
	- The theoretical probability of an outcome is the relative frequency if we repeated the action *infinitely* many times
- **Empirical probabilities** are based on outcomes of an experiment or by observing the real-life process
- When experiments with real-life processes are too expensive or time-consuming, we can sometimes rely on **simulations** of those real-life processes
